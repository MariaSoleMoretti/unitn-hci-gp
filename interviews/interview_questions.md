# Interview Questions

## Intro
Hi! First of all, thank you so much for taking the time to speak with me today.

My name is [Your Name], and I am conducting research for a Human-Computer Interaction project at University of Trento. The goal of my study is to better understand how people experience monitoring their mental well-being through digital tools, so that we can design technology that is more aligned with actual user needs.

Before we begin, I’d like to reassure you on a few key points:

- **There are no right or wrong answers**: I am only interested in your honest experience and unique perspective.

- **Privacy & Ethics**: All data collected today will be treated with total anonymity and used exclusively for academic purposes related to this project. Your name will never be associated with any statements you make.

- **Right to Withdraw**: You are free to stop the interview at any time or skip any question if you do not feel comfortable.

Thank you again for having already signed the informed consent. As stated in the document, your data will remain anonymous and protected. Before we officially begin, I would like to ask for your permission to record the audio of this interview to help with my data analysis. Is it okay if I start the recording now?

## Questions
### Warm up
- To start off, what do you currently do? (Are you a student, working, or something else?)
- In your daily life, which apps or digital tools do you find yourself using most frequently on your smartphone?
- Generally speaking, how would you describe your relationship with technology? Are you someone who loves trying out the latest gadgets and apps right away, or do you prefer waiting until they are more established?"

### Intro questions
- What tools or methods, if any, have you used in the past to track your mood? Could you tell me more about which specific one you used?
- If you had to choose between a digital app and a physical paper journal for mood tracking, which format would you prefer? What makes that format more appealing to you?
- What made you start using it?
- If you stopped using it, why?

### What users’ experiences were like, what was memorable, and why
- How would you describe the process of logging your mood at the end of the day?
- In your opinion, what frequency of entries would best represent your emotional trends?
- How do you feel about using emojis, numbers, or scales to describe mood?

### Users’ pain points during an experience
- How would you describe your level of consistency with mood tracking over time?
- What factors, if any, influence your ability to maintain a regular tracking routine?"
- How effectively does the current system allow you to capture the full range of your emotions?
- If you could design a mood-tracking tool tailored for you own needs, what would it do?

### Users’ mental models
- Can you walk me though the process of recording your moods? 

### Users’ motivations, aspirations, and desires
- What are you hoping to achieve by using mood-tracking in the long run?
- What motivates you to keep tracking your emotions even when it becomes difficult?

### Question for our solution
Imagine a system that, instead of asking you to write a daily journal, allows you to record a short voice note or a quick video of your day. At the same time, the system uses data from your smartwatch (such as heart rate or physical activity) to analyze your well-being. The goal is to provide you with an automated overview of your stress and mood, interpreting your data without requiring you to fill in anything manually.

- What are your thoughts on using voice input compared to text input for logging your data?
- How do you feel about the opportunity of recording youself/your voice to infer you mood?
- If there were an option to record a video of your mood, how would you compare the experience of reading a text transcript versus rewatching the video recording?
- Would you trust AI to help interpret your emotional state? Why or why not?
- In what ways might guided prompts influence how you document your emotional state?
- Regarding any biometric devices you use: how often do you consult your data, and for what reasons?
- What are your thoughts regarding privacy in relation to the use of video or biometric data?

## Closing
Reflecting on our conversation, is there anything else you’d like to add before we conclude?
