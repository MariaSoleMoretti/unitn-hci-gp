# Interview Questions

## Intro
We are designing a mood-tracking system using short videos, smartwatch data, and AI support. We want to understand your habits, needs, and concerns. There are no right or wrong answers.

## Questions
### Intro questions
- What tools or methods, if any, have you used in the past to track your mood? Could you tell me more about which specific one you used?
- If you had to choose between a digital app and a physical paper journal for mood tracking, which format would you prefer? What makes that format more appealing to you?
- What made you start using it?
- If you stopped using it, why?

### What users’ experiences were like, what was memorable, and why
- How would you describe the process of logging your mood at the end of the day?
- In your opinion, what frequency of entries would best represent your emotional trends?
- How do you feel about using emojis, numbers, or scales to describe mood?

### Users’ pain points during an experience
- How would you describe your level of consistency with mood tracking over time?
- What factors, if any, influence your ability to maintain a regular tracking routine?"
- How effectively does the current system allow you to capture the full range of your emotions?
- If you could design a mood-tracking tool tailored for you own needs, what would it do?

### Users’ mental models
- Can you walk me though the process of recording your moods? 

### Users’ motivations, aspirations, and desires
- What are you hoping to achieve by using mood-tracking in the long run?
- What motivates you to keep tracking your emotions even when it becomes difficult?

### Question for our solution
- What are your thoughts on using voice input compared to text input for logging your data?
- How do you feel about the opportunity of recording youself/your voice to infer you mood?
- If there were an option to record a video of your mood, how would you compare the experience of reading a text transcript versus rewatching the video recording?
- Would you trust AI to help interpret your emotional state? Why or why not?
- In what ways might guided prompts influence how you document your emotional state?
- Regarding any biometric devices you use: how often do you consult your data, and for what reasons?
- What are your thoughts regarding privacy in relation to the use of video or biometric data?

## Closing
Reflecting on our conversation, is there anything else you’d like to add before we conclude?
