# Interview Questions

## Intro
We are designing a mood-tracking system using short videos, smartwatch data, and AI support. We want to understand your habits, needs, and concerns. There are no right or wrong answers.

## Questions
1. How do you usually notice that your mood has changed?

2. Do you do anything to reflect on or track your emotions now?

3. Have you ever used a mood-tracking app or journal? Which one?

4. What made you start using it?

5. What worked well?

6. What did not work well?

7. If you stopped using it, why?

8. Does logging mood at the end of the day feel difficult or inaccurate? Why?

9. Do you feel one entry per day is enough to represent your emotions?

10. How do you feel about using emojis, numbers, or scales to describe mood?

11. What would make mood tracking genuinely useful for you?

12. What kind of feedback would you want: charts, summaries, suggestions, or something else?

13. What is your reaction to recording short videos to track mood?

14. Would speaking to a system feel easier or harder than writing?

15. Would guided questions help you express your feelings?

16. How do you feel about using smartwatch data like heart rate or sleep as extra context?

17. Would you trust AI to help interpret your emotional state? Why or why not?

18. Would reading a transcript feel easier than rewatching a negative video?

19. What privacy concerns would you have with video or biometric data?

20. If you could design the ideal mood-tracking tool, what would it do?

## Closing
Is there anything important we did not ask?
