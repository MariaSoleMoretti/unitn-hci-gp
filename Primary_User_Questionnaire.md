### Barriers & Disengagement

1\. Emotional Barriers and Selective Tracking  
**Aversion to Negative States:** Many users feel a strong disinclination to document negative moods. Instead of using the app during difficult moments, they often prefer to record only positive experiences to avoid the "guilt" or discomfort of seeing their struggles visualized.  
*EN*: "I tend to avoid using mood-tracking apps during difficult moments because I dislike seeing my struggles or negative moods visualized."

**The "Cry vs. Log" Paradox:** Users reported that when they are feeling particularly bad, they would rather "just lay there and cry" than exert the effort to open an app and log their distress.

2\. The Interpretation and Action Gap

**Lack of Actionable Advice:** A major shortfall of these apps is that they often provide "confirmation of feeling shitty" without offering solutions. Users frequently complained that apps do not provide facilitated recommendations or suggestions on how to improve their mood based on the data provided.

3\. Inaccurate Representation of Mood Dynamics

**Daily Fluctuations**: Mood is highly dynamic and can change multiple times within an hour. However, many apps encourage or only allow one entry per day, which fails to capture these fluctuations and can ignore significant shifts that occur after the daily log is completed.

4\. Challenges with Data Review and Summaries  
**Misleading Aggregations:** Users were often critical of review features like pie charts or stacked bar graphs that "label" an entire month based on general trends. These summaries can feel unrepresentative or "wrong" because they erase the specific nuances of individual days.  
*EN*: "I find that monthly summaries (like pie charts) often feel 'wrong' or unrepresentative because they erase the specific nuances of my individual days."

5\. Burden and Personalization Tensions  
**High Tracking Burden**: The effort required to manually enter data leads to infrequent use, which undermines the app's ability to provide a complete record,.  
**Personalization vs. Simplicity**: There is a constant tension between the desire for a simple, low-burden interface and the need for enough customization to accurately reflect an individual's unique emotional vocabulary
